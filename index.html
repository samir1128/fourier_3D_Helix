This is the unified picture. Merge the 3D helix interpretation of einωt with the 2D epicycle view of Fourier sums. What you are seeing is both geometry and algebra at once.

In full mode, the outer tip traces the compound path. Switch to front view to collapse time and reveal epicycles. Switch to side view to reveal the real-valued signal emerging from summed cosines.

Head-to-tail in 3D means each new helix is attached to the moving tip of the previous one. So every smaller spiral rides on top of a larger moving spiral.

The 3D helix and the 2D epicycle are the same object viewed from different angles. Fourier series says periodic signals are shadows of rotating nested spirals.
